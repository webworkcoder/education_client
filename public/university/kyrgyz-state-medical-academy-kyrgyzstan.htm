About Kyrgyz State Medical Academy: Kyrgyzstan

Kyrgyz State Medical Academy (KSMA) is a public medical university located in Bishkek, Kyrgyzstan. Founded in 1939, it is one of the oldest and most prestigious medical institutions in Kyrgyzstan.

The University offers undergraduate and postgraduate programs in medicine, dentistry, nursing, and pharmacy, with instruction in both English and Russian. KSMA has a modern curriculum that emphasizes research, clinical skills, and community-oriented healthcare. KSMA is recognized by the Ministry of Education and Science of the Kyrgyz Republic and is also listed in the World Health Organization (WHO) directory of medical schools.

The university is also a member of the International Association of Universities, the Association of Medical Schools of Asia, and the Association for Medical Education in Europe. The university has a diverse student population, with students from different countries such as India, Pakistan, Nepal, Turkey, and others. KSMA offers a multicultural environment that fosters cultural exchange and encourages students to appreciate and learn from different cultures. KSMA has well-equipped laboratories, libraries, and modern facilities providing students with an excellent learning environment. Additionally, the university offers comfortable accommodation for its students in its dormitories, which are equipped with all the essential amenities.

Why To Study in Kyrgyzstan?

  3. Cultural experience: Studying in Kyrgyzstan can be a unique cultural experience for international students. The country has a rich history and diverse population, and students can learn about its culture and traditions while studying there.
  4. Language opportunities: Kyrgyzstan is a multilingual country where Russian and Kyrgyz are the official languages. Studying in Kyrgyzstan can provide students with the opportunity to learn or improve their language skills in one or both of these languages.
  5. Location: Kyrgyzstan is located in Central Asia, with borders to Kazakhstan, China, Tajikistan, and Uzbekistan. This location provides students with the opportunity to explore other parts of Central Asia and beyond. Additionally, the country is known for its beautiful landscapes and outdoor activities, providing students with plenty of opportunities to explore and enjoy nature.

Eligibility:

  • To be eligible for medical education at Kyrgyz State Medical Academy, candidates are required to have completed their higher secondary examination with Science subjects including Physics, Chemistry, and Biology.
  • Additionally, they should have obtained an aggregate of 50% or more in PCB in their 12th standard results. It is mandatory for students to be at least 17 years of age to pursue medical education at Kyrgyz State Medical Academy.
  • For Indian students who plan to study MBBS abroad, it is compulsory to clear the NEET exams.

Application Process:

  • To secure admission to Kyrgyz State Medical Academy, prospective students are required to follow a series of steps.
  • The first step involves completing an online application form provided by the Kyrgyz State Medical Academy, where the course registration form must be filled out.
  • Once the form is filled, applicants must print it out, sign it, and submit it along with the necessary documents listed by the university within the given deadline.
  • These documents should be scanned and emailed to the admissions office with the subject line 'Application for Admission.'
  • It is crucial to submit all the required documents before the final submission date. After Kyrgyz State Medical Academy verifies the student's documents, the student will receive an acceptance or rejection letter accordingly.
  • Once accepted, the student must pay the initial fees, and upon receiving the acknowledgment letter, the student will receive their fee.
  • It is also essential to apply for a student visa immediately after being accepted, and the university will provide Invitation letters to the students.
